2. On-site Audit

The field audit plan is largely divided into general controls (testing the environment and operations) and applied controls. This point must be distinguished and judged carefully.

Control Evaluation - Assess whether controls are appropriate for the current situation or environment

General Controls (Conformity Testing) - Test the organization's environment overall. - Test compliance with rules or whether the rules are appropriate (conformity test)

Application Control (Verification Testing) - Verify the quality of actual performance. - Prove that controls are not being maintained well. - Verify the integrity of actual processing. - Verify the results. - Actually try it. (ex: Check whether the financial statement report is correct)

3. Report

- Consult with the auditee. Explain the facts about the findings and seek consent. - A closing meeting must be held with the auditor and the audit report must be distributed.

4. Follow-up

* This appeared on the test

- Review follow-up actions for recommendations to determine whether management's corrective actions have achieved their objectives

- If management decides to take a risk, there is no need for documentation or follow-up.

If it turns out to be a problem) is The auditor decided to find out about management's corrective actions. What is the correct purpose for doing this? -> This is to determine whether the risk was successfully reduced or eliminated by accepting the auditor's recommendations and carrying out corrective actions.

7. Types of Control

* Must appear in the exam

Preventive Control - Control to prevent problems before they occur - Separation of work functions (duty separation, work rotation), physical access control, transaction approval procedures, etc.

Detection and Control - Controls to detect and report problems - Hash sum, checkpoint, audit, access log

Correctional Control - Controls to minimize threats and restore the system to normal afterwards - Reduced risk due to BCP, recovery and backup, and remediation * Remember that recovery and backup are included with BCP.

Complementary Controls - Control that complements existing system controls to compensate for potential or existing risks - Reduce inherent risks through complementary controls ex) If there is an existing vulnerable module that cannot be replaced, find a patch that will resolve the vulnerability of that module.

* Governance is unconditionally the responsibility of the board of directors * Management responsibility is top management and executives

9. Audit Testing

Control Test (Conformity Test) - Verify that control procedures are being followed as defined in advance - Verify compliance with rules and whether they are effective. - This is a test to verify the environment. - Use attribute sampling.

Procedure

  1. Investigation
  2. Query
  3. Re-do
  4. Follow-up (walk-through) - Backtrack the overall business process

Empirical Test - This is a test that verifies the integrity of actual processing. - Tests the integrity of a specific processing or application program (minor part). - Use random sampling.

10. Audit Sampling

Statistical Sampling - The number of errors can be objectively quantified. Have objective standards.

Random Sampling - Randomly sample with random numbers

Systematic Sampling - The first sample is selected randomly, and subsequent samples are selected by serial number.

Non-Statistical Sampling - Just extract it randomly

Attribute Sampling - Conformity testing, used for general control - Divided into intermittent sampling and discovery sampling. - Intermittent sampling: Intermittent sampling is performed again until the error probability of the sample population is lower than the expected result. Then, it ends when a population that meets the expected results is found. - Discovery sampling: Sampling that discovers errors or irregularities.

변량 샘플링 - Verification testing, application control - Verify that the results from actual processing are flawless.

Stratified Sampling - Divide the population according to specific criteria. Because the population from which to extract is limited, the sample size also becomes small.

13. IS Code of Professional Ethics

1. ISACA establishes this Code of Professional Ethics to provide guidelines for the professional and personal conduct of association members and certificate holders. 2. ISACA members and certificate holders must comply with the following regulations. 1) Effective governance and management of a company's information systems and technology, as well as the establishment of appropriate standards and procedures applicable to auditing, control, security, and risk management, must be supported and compliance promoted. 2) Duties must be performed with objectivity, sincere diligence, and professional care in accordance with job standards. 3) Maintain high standards of conduct and dignity, do not undermine the trust of your profession or association, and contribute to the interests of stakeholders in a legal manner. 4) The privacy and confidentiality of information acquired during the performance of work must be maintained, unless disclosure is requested by judicial authorities. The information must not be used for personal purposes or provided to inappropriate parties. 5) You must agree to perform a task only if you remain skilled in your field of expertise and can complete the task using the necessary skills, knowledge and abilities. 6) All important facts that may distort the result report if not disclosed must be disclosed and the results of the work performed must be delivered to the appropriate target (delivered to the audited department, not to other departments!). 7) Support job training for stakeholders by improving their understanding of governance and management of corporate information systems and technology, as well as audit, control, security, and risk management.

14. Governance

Corporate Governance: Control to achieve corporate objectives

Information Security Governance: Aims to protect and improve operational efficiency.

IT Governance: Strategic Alignment - Providing value (profit-based) - Performance Measurement (Measuring IT Performance with BSC) -> This is to share the organization’s strategy. - Resource management - Risk Management -> Management is aware of risk -> The ideal situation is to understand the organization’s appetite for risk. -> Responsibility always lies with the organization. - Compliance (Policy Management)

15. The Role of IT Governance Audits

Role 1. Always monitor policies, laws, and regulations. 2. Review IT governance and identify areas for improvement. 3. If there are any exemplary cases, recommend them to management. 4. Helps ensure policy compliance. 5. Evaluate from an independent and objective perspective.

Purpose Pursued Strategic linkage between business and IT - Effective IT Governance 1. Demonstrate leadership and ensure 2. Ensure the structure of the organization well. 3. Helping the process run well
