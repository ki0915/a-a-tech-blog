BCP (IT Governance and Management - 1 > ^94d4cb)

BCP (Business Continuity Planning)

  • Protect essential business processes.
  • The organization is more important than the customer.
  • Testing is usually structural walkthroughs (document testing).
  • However, actual testing is the most reliable.

BIA (Business Impact Analysis)

  • Identify critical business processes and determine recovery times and documentation methods.

DRP (Disaster Recovery Planning)

  • Protect essential business processes.

Commonalities between BCP and DRP

  • Accepts risk.
  • Emphasizes availability.
  • Targets residual risk.
  • Treated as confidential.

COOP (Continuity of Operations Plan)

  • Strategy for operating from a secondary site for up to 30 days.

DEP (Disaster Emergency Plan)

  • Focuses on saving lives.
  • Saving people is the highest priority.

Secondary Sites

Mirror

  • Everything is identical, including data.

Hot

  • Everything is identical except data.

Warm

  • Some equipment is available.

Cold

  • Almost nothing is available.

Types of Ownership for Secondary Sites

Owned

  • Implemented by the organization itself.

Joint Investment

  • Co-invested to implement a secondary site.
  • Challenges include determining priority vendors during recovery.

Commercial Service

  • Purchase secondary site services.
  • Security and reliability are crucial.

Reciprocal Agreements

  • Implemented through mutual agreements between companies.
  • The least expensive option.

Considerations When Building Secondary Sites

  • Should not be susceptible to the same disaster as the primary site.
  • Requires appropriate hardware and software compatibility.
  • Monitor workload.
  • Agree on recovery priorities.
  • Testing is mandatory.
