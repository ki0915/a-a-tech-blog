Information Processing Facility (IPF)

- IPF refers to all computer hardware, software, and data. - These include data stored on hard drives or USBs.

DBA (Database Administrator)

- Database management is one of the most important aspects for an auditor to consider.
Why is database management one of the most important aspects for an auditor to consider?

Because the DBA has access to all data.

  • DBAs must enforce strict separation of duties.
  • All actions performed by the DBA must be approved by management.
  • The DBA is responsible for defining and managing backups and recovery.
  • By controlling updates and concurrency, the DBA ensures data integrity.

Important Notes

  • Quality assurance, development/design/maintenance, and operations roles cannot be held concurrently by the same individual.
  • DBA, quality manager, security manager, and roles in development/design/maintenance and operations cannot be held concurrently by the same individual.

Selecting an Outsourcing Vendor

  • Suitable vendors are selected through a benchmark RFI (Request for Information) process.
  • If a suitable vendor is found, an RFP (Request for Proposal) is sent to request a proposal.
  • Based on the proposals received from the candidate vendors, the most suitable vendor is selected.

* It's okay not to choose just one vendor.
* It's okay to select multiple vendors and choose one as the main vendor.

Completeness Checks, Accuracy Checks, and Corresponding Controls

Completeness Check

Checks whether data is missing or if there are any duplicates.
**e.g., parity check, run-to-run checks, transaction logs, badge registers

Accuracy Check

  • Checks to determine if the data is accurate.
    * e.g., check digits, rationality checks

Controls Corresponding to Each Check – This is guaranteed to appear on the exam.
