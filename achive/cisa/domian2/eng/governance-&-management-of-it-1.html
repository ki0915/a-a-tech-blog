Concepts Appearing on the Exam

Business Continuity (BCP) and Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

BIA (Business Impact Analysis)

Business Impact Analysis (BIA) is the process of analyzing the importance of business processes or components. It helps in disaster recovery or continuity planning by determining which processes to recover first and how to recover them.

DRP (Disaster Recovery Plan)

Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) is a plan to recover a company's IT systems and data in the event of a disaster or incident. DRP clarifies recovery procedures and responsibilities, and defines the resources and procedures for system recovery.

RTO (Recovery Time Objective) and RPO (Recovery Point Objective)

RTO (Recovery Time Objective)

Recovery Time Objective (RTO) is the maximum acceptable time for a system or service to be restored after a disruption. It serves as a crucial criterion for setting recovery goals.

RPO (Recovery Point Objective)

Recovery Point Objective (RPO) is the maximum allowable period of data loss. It defines the point in time to which data must be recovered following a disruption.

CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility)

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) is the concept that a company should operate ethically and sustainably, taking responsibility for its impact on society and the environment. CSR includes contributing positively to the community and engaging in environmental protection.

Committee Roles Explained

IT Strategy Committee

The IT Strategy Committee focuses on setting the IT strategic direction and establishing long-term goals. This committee ensures that IT strategies align with the organization’s business objectives and develops strategic plans to address future IT environment changes.

  • Setting IT strategic direction
  • Establishing long-term IT goals and plans
  • Ensuring alignment between IT strategy and business objectives

The IT Strategy Committee must maintain official minutes for all meetings.

IT Operations Committee

The IT Operations Committee manages and oversees the daily operations related to IT systems. This committee focuses on practical aspects such as system monitoring, budget approval, and resource allocation to enhance operational efficiency.

  • Monitoring and maintaining IT systems
  • Approving budgets and allocating resources
  • Managing daily IT operations

The IT Operations Committee must also maintain official minutes for all meetings.

IT Concepts Explained

IT BSC (Balanced Scorecard)
  • A tool to measure the efficiency of an organization's IT.
  • Demonstrates the effectiveness of IT.
  • Used as a reporting tool for the board of directors.
  • A means to share organizational strategy.
EA (Enterprise Architecture)
  • The process of structurally documenting all organizational assets.
  • Helps guide investments and efficiently manage assets.
When should EA be used? When deciding on investment direction.
Process Maturity (CMM)
  • Initial: A stage with simple development and no processes.
  • Repeatable: Processes are consistently repeated, with processes for projects.
  • Defined: Processes are fully defined.
  • Managed: Data for processes is automatically collected, allowing for comprehensive process improvements.
  • Optimizing: The stage where processes can be improved qualitatively and quantitatively.

IT Investment and Resource Allocation Practices

Portfolio Management

  • 1. Strategy Development -> 2. Investment -> Execute starting with the highest priorities.

Investment Management

  • 1. Business Case -> Understand the business case.
  • 2. Program Planning -> Execute the program.

Various Concepts (Risk Management)

Quantitative Analysis Techniques

  • Expresses the probability of risks numerically and objectively.
  • Mainly used in Business Impact Analysis (BIA).

Important Aspects of Risk Management

  • The responsibility for risk management lies with management.
  • Quantitative risk management is more accurate than qualitative risk management.
  • Special care is needed for high-impact events, even if the probability is low.

Information System Management Practices

Human Resource Management

Separation of Duties
  • Excessive separation of duties can be counterproductive.
  • Large companies find it easier to separate duties, but smaller companies may face challenges due to financial constraints.
  • Should be considered appropriately based on the situation.
Cross-Training
  • Helps maintain operational continuity.
  • Employees learn additional tasks while performing their main duties.
  • May increase risk vulnerability.
Mandatory Leave
  • Can prevent or detect employee fraud or misconduct.
  • Employees should be given mandatory leave at least once a year, and their duties should be performed by someone else during this period. For departing employees, all access rights should be immediately revoked or deleted.

Job Descriptions

  • Details the requirements for performing a job rather than roles and responsibilities.
  • Access to specific areas of work is based on job descriptions.

Performance Measurement Metrics

  • Critical Success Factors (CSFs)
  • Key Goal Indicators (KGIs) -> Measured at the beginning of the year: Setting our goals.
  • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) -> Measured at the end of the year: Evaluating goal achievement.

Metrics used to assess how well objectives are achieved.

Maturity Models

A technique for evaluating IT functions or processes through process management assessments.

Benchmarking

A systematic method of comparing performance with peers and competitors to identify best practices.
